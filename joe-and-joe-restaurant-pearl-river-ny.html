Some restaurants are just places to eat. And then there's Joe & Joe — a name that has meant family, tradition, and real Italian-American cooking for over 80 years. From the streets of the Bronx to the heart of Pearl River, this is a story worth telling.

The History

It started in the Bronx in 1940, when two men named Giuseppe — Giuseppe Perrotta and Giuseppe Bevilacqua — purchased an old establishment on Castle Hill Avenue and gave it a name that would last for generations: Joe & Joe. For 64 years, it was a cornerstone of the East Bronx — feeding families, hosting community groups, and building a reputation for Italian cooking that had no equal in the neighborhood.

1940
The Bronx Original OpensTwo Giuseppes — Perrotta and Bevilacqua — launch Joe & Joe on Castle Hill Ave, East Bronx. A legend is born.
2000
Pearl River OpensJoseph Longobardo, grandson of the original founders, brings Joe & Joe to 65 E Central Ave in Pearl River, carrying the tradition to Rockland County.
2004
Bronx Location ClosesAfter 64 years, the original Bronx restaurant closes its doors — but the Pearl River location carries the flame forward.
Today
Still Going StrongJoe & Joe Pearl River remains one of Rockland County's most beloved Italian restaurants — entertainment Friday & Saturday nights, full lunch and dinner service daily.
The Food

Every item is prepared to order using only the finest ingredients. Bountiful portions of classic Italian-American dishes — mozzarella carrozza, baked clams, stuffed sole, homemade lasagna packed with tiny meatballs, and more. As owner Joseph Longobardo puts it: "food so good, you'll expect grandma to walk out of the kitchen."

"My grandfather opened the original Joe & Joe in the Bronx in 1940. In August of 2000 I decided to open Joe & Joe in the heart of Pearl River. My hope was to bring authentic old fashion Italian cuisine to Rockland County."
— Joseph Longobardo, Owner

Why Pearl River Loves It

Pearl River has no shortage of good spots to eat — but Joe & Joe sits in a category of its own. It's not just the food, which has drawn rave reviews for decades. It's the feeling you get when you walk in. The same family hospitality that made the Bronx original legendary for 64 years is alive and well on East Central Avenue. Whether it's a weeknight dinner or a Saturday night out with live entertainment, Joe & Joe delivers every time.

And if you haven't been yet — or haven't been in a while — now is a great time to go. Pearl River's dining scene is as strong as ever, and this is one of the anchors that makes it special.
