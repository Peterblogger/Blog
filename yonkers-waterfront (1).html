How We Got Here

A Century of Sugar — Now Something New

1893
The Sugar House Opens
The Domino Sugar Refinery at 1 Federal Street begins operations on the Yonkers waterfront — a fixture of the city for over 130 years.
JUNE 2025
Closure Announced
American Sugar Refining announces the refinery will shut down by end of 2025. About 300–350 workers are affected. Mayor Spano calls the site "one of the most valuable properties from here to Albany."
DEC 2025
Doors Close for Good
The refinery officially closes. Yonkers City Council expands downtown mixed-use zoning to include the 33-acre parcel, clearing the path for redevelopment.
APRIL 2026
The Refinery District Hits the Market
Cushman & Wakefield is retained to market the site — now branded The Refinery District. It's the last large-scale development opportunity on the Yonkers waterfront.

From the Agent

The Last Big Piece of the Yonkers Waterfront Just Opened Up

If you've been watching Yonkers for any amount of time, you already know this waterfront has been transforming for years. New buildings, new restaurants, new energy. But there was always this one massive piece that hadn't moved — the old Domino Sugar refinery sitting on 33 acres right on the Hudson. That just changed.

American Sugar Refining closed the plant at the end of 2025 after more than 130 years of operation. It wasn't easy for the community — around 300 workers lost their jobs and this was a building most Yonkers residents simply called the Sugar House. But what comes next is what every buyer, seller, and investor in Westchester needs to understand right now.

"One of the most valuable properties from here to Albany." — Yonkers Mayor Mike Spano

Cushman & Wakefield has been retained to market the site — now called The Refinery District — and the numbers are staggering. We're talking about potential for roughly 2,650 residential units, 2.6 million square feet of development, over 2,300 linear feet of Hudson River frontage, and a location that puts you in Midtown Manhattan in under 30 minutes on Metro-North. The city has already rezoned the land for mixed-use development. The path is clear.

Think about what happened to Brooklyn's waterfront when development moved in. Property values in surrounding neighborhoods moved first — before the buildings were even built. We are at that moment right now in Yonkers. The buyers who position themselves in this market today are the ones who look smart in five years.
